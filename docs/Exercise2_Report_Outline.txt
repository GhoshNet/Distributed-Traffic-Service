CS7NS6 Distributed Systems 2025-2026

Exercise 2

Implementing a Globally-accessible Distributed Service

Final Report Outline

The final report should include chapters addressing the following
issues:

1.  Requirements: Describe the functionality provided and outline the
    functional and non-functional requirements addressed in its design.

2.  Specification: Provide a detailed specification of the functionality
    provided by your system, including:

-   The services it is composed of
-   Their APIs
-   System behaviour in the presence of faults

Ensure consistency with what you demonstrate. Clearly state any
requirements that are not fully addressed.

3.  Architecture and Design: Describe the system’s technical and
    software architecture.

-   Explain runtime services and how they are distributed/connected
-   Each team member must describe the design of their most significant
    service

4.  Implementation: Describe system operation, including:

-   Behavioural diagrams for key algorithms
-   Handling of important failure modes
-   Algorithms such as: • Failure detection
    • Consensus on group membership
    • Partition tolerance

5.  Testing: Outline:

-   Test plan
-   Test results

6.  Allocation of Work: Describe how work was distributed among team
    members.

7.  Summary: Summarise overall achievements.

Additional Notes:

-   Maximum length: 20 pages (can be shorter)
-   Use diagrams where appropriate
-   Report must be signed by all group members
